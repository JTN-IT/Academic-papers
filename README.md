Papers and reports, 2009–2020

A collection of academic work in physical geography, GIS, and remote sensing, produced at the University of Amsterdam (BSc, MSc) and the University of Aberdeen (MSc). Subjects range from glacial geomorphology and plant physiology to water quality assessment, computer vision, agricultural land mapping, and the philosophy of science.

They are collected here as a record rather than a showcase — coursework, essays, and project reports, of varying scope and formality, kept in their original form.

The subjects vary widely, but a consistent way of working runs through them. Each is, in one form or another, concerned with how you reach a defensible conclusion when the evidence is incomplete: making assumptions explicit, weighing criteria against one another, testing hypotheses by trying to disprove them rather than confirm them, and stating plainly where the analysis runs out. The Iran assessment sets that out most directly, using the Analysis of Competing Hypotheses method; the essay on reductionism argues the case from the other end, that condensing a phenomenon into any single discipline's language loses information that interdisciplinary work retains. The rest apply the same approach to particular problems.

Tools and methods used across the collection include ArcMap, QGIS, R, Erdas Imagine, NDVI and supervised classification of remote sensing imagery, multi-criteria evaluation and pairwise weighting, spline interpolation, image segmentation and computer vision, and the Analysis of Competing Hypotheses.


**[Analysis Report Iran](./2009%20Analysis%20Report%20Iran.pdf)**
Structured intelligence assessment of Iran's nuclear intentions, capability, and deployment likelihood using the Analysis of Competing Hypotheses method.
Could Iran build and use a nuclear weapon? — working through the evidence with a formal intelligence-analysis method to test rival explanations against each other (BSc coursework, UvA 2009)

**[Glacial Landscape Evolution of the Patagonian Icefields](./2009%20Glacial%20Landscape%20Evolution%20of%20the%20Patagonian%20Icefields.pdf)**
Review of non-climatic controls on Patagonian ice-sheet dynamics — landform mapping vs. numerical models.
Why Patagonia's ice sheets don't match the models — how the shape of the land, not just climate, drove glacier behaviour (BSc coursework, UvA 2009)
 
**[Plant response to elevated carbon dioxide levels](./2009%20Plant%20response%20to%20elevated%20carbon%20dioxide%20levels.pdf)**
Plant response to elevated CO₂ — survey of biochemical adaptations across C3/C4/CAM photosynthetic pathways and nitrogen cycling.
What rising CO₂ actually does to plants — how the three main photosynthesis types respond, and why more carbon doesn't simply mean more growth (BSc coursework, UvA 2009)

**[Analysis of the Seine-Normandy River Basin](./2014%20Analysis%20of%20the%20Seine-Normandy%20River%20Basin.pdf)**
Assessment of the Seine-Normandy river basin against EU Water Framework Directive criteria — hydromorphology, water body typology, ecological and chemical status, with a case study of a heavily modified segment downstream of Paris.
How healthy is the river Seine, and how do you actually measure that? — applying the EU's water quality rules to France's biggest river basin, including a close look at one badly polluted stretch below Paris (group project, UvA Water Quality, 2014)

**[Reductionism and Earth Science](./2014%20Reductionism%20and%20Earth%20Science.pdf)**
Philosophy of science essay arguing that Earth Science, as an inherently interdisciplinary study of natural phenomena, cannot be reduced to physics, chemistry or biology — and that the reduction runs the other way.
Can Earth Science be boiled down to physics and chemistry? — an argument that it can't, because studying nature across disciplines gets closer to reality than any single discipline's language can (Vrije Universiteit, 2014)

**[A Land Use Mapping Project at Knock Farm](./2019%20A%20Land%20Use%20Mapping%20Project%20at%20Knock%20Farm.pdf)**
Commercial GIS tender project mapping agricultural land use across a 400ha Aberdeenshire farm — field boundary digitisation in ArcMap, James Hutton Institute soil and land capability datasets, spline interpolation of soil chemistry sampling points, NDVI analysis of CIR aerial imagery, and attempted supervised classification in Erdas Imagine.
Mapping a Scottish farm to find out which fields grow what best — and finding out the available data couldn't answer the question (MSc GIS, University of Aberdeen, 2019)

**[Computer vision for histogram analysis of grape berry clusters in R](./2020%20Computer%20vision%20for%20histogram%20analysis%20of%20grape%20berry%20clusters%20in%20R.pdf)**
Computer vision pipeline in R extracting per-channel colour histograms from RGB vineyard imagery as a ripeness proxy — histogram equalisation, SLICO superpixel segmentation with affinity propagation clustering, custom RGB range filtering, and inverse masking.
Can a robot tell when grapes are ripe just by looking? — building a tool that finds berry clusters in a photo and measures their colour, as a working example of how vineyard robots would report back to the farmer (MSc GIS mini project, University of Aberdeen, 2020)

**[Multi-Criteria Evaluation in GIS](./2020%20Multi-Criteria%20Evaluation%20in%20GIS.pdf)**
Poster covering the full MCE workflow in GIS — objective definition, Boolean and continuous criteria surfaces, Saaty pairwise comparison and eigenvector weighting, ordered weighting and the risk/trade-off decision strategy space, suitability mapping, and site selection.
How do you choose the best place to put something? — a walkthrough of the GIS method for weighing up competing demands (environment, cost, access, politics) and turning them into a map of where a project should actually go (MSc GIS, University of Aberdeen, 2020)

**[Operations and Maintenance Manual for VLE](./2020%20Operations%20and%20Maintenance%20Manual%20for%20VLE.pdf)**
Implementation and handover documentation for the VLE database — physical model in MySQL DDL with primary/foreign key constraints and ON DELETE/ON UPDATE policies graded by data sensitivity, MyISAM engine rationale, READ COMMITTED isolation with autocommit disabled, role-based access privilege design, example queries and populated test data, plus backup, journaling, service and lifecycle recommendations.
The manual that comes with the database — how to run it, query it, back it up, control who can see what, and hand it on to whoever maintains it next (MSc Database Systems and Big Data, University of Aberdeen, 2020)
